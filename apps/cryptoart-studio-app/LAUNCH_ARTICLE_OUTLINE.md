# Launch Article Outline: "Welcome to Cryptoart.Social: Your Home for Art Discovery and Creation"

## Article Structure

### 1. Opening: What You'll See
**Experience Focus:** Paint a picture of landing on the page for the first time

- The first thing you see: a featured 1/1 auction with beautiful art
- The hero section: art on the left, value proposition on the right
- Community stats visible immediately (X collectors, Y artists, Z ETH volume)
- The feeling: "This is about art, not trading"

**Key Points:**
- Art is the primary UI element
- Everything is designed to showcase work
- Stats provide context without overwhelming
- Clear paths forward (browse, bid, create)

---

### 2. Browse & Discover: Exploring the Marketplace
**Experience Focus:** Walk through the discovery experience

- Featured Artist Showcase: rotating horizontal scroll of artists getting traction
- Featured 1/1 Auctions: grid of high-quality pieces currently live
- All Auctions: unified marketplace (featured and community together)
- Recognition Row: see who's making an impact (top curator, most supported artist)
- Featured NFT Pools: preview of liquidity pools on Such.Market

**Key Points:**
- Multiple ways to discover art
- Featured content rotates (keeps it fresh)
- Community auctions get equal treatment in design
- Recognition is visible but not dominant

**User Actions:**
- Click through to auction details
- View artist galleries
- Follow curators
- Explore pools

**Key Points:**

**User Actions:**
- Deploy collection (if needed)

## Writing Tips

### Tone
- Positive, welcoming, experience-focused
- No comparisons to other platforms
- Focus on what users can do and experience
- Emphasize quality and curation

### Structure
- Each section should feel like walking through the experience
- Use "you" language to make it personal
- Include specific examples of what users see and do
- Balance detail with readability

### Key Messages
- Art comes first
- Tools enable creation, curation, and collecting
- Quality work gets recognition
- Community drives success
- Everyone can participate

### Call to Action
- End with invitation to explore
- Link to the platform
- Encourage trying different paths (artist, curator, collector)
- Emphasize that it's free to browse and participate

---

## Article Length
- Target: 1,500-2,000 words
- Each section: 200-300 words
- Keep it scannable with clear sections
- Use subheadings and bullet points where helpful
